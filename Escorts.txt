Orchestrator
Declaration: The original, main reasoning agent.
Function: Designed to interpret a user prompt and generate a single, optimal pipeline of performers to accomplish the task.
Key Revealed Gaps:
Lack of Creativity (Innovate, Propose): A glorified pattern-matcher. It could assemble known performers but couldn't innovate a new one, nor could it propose multiple, distinct strategic options.
Amnesia (Store, Recall): Fundamentally stateless. It had no memory of past successes or failures, treating every task as a brand-new problem.
No Oversight (Escalate): It was a "fire and forget" planner. It had no awareness of the pipeline's execution and couldn't detect or escalate a real-time failure.
Naive Simplicity (Harmonize, Attune, Refute): It was a naive optimist. It assumed performers would work perfectly together, had no ability to fine-tune their parameters, and couldn't question or refute the premise of a user's prompt.
The Strategist
Declaration: The system's multi-option planner.
Function: Takes a user prompt and generates a set of distinct, viable plans, explicitly contrasting their trade-offs (e.g., speed vs. accuracy) to inform a better decision.
Key Revealed Gaps:
Indecisiveness (Select): Its core function is to propose, not to choose. It creates the menu but lacks the authority or logic to select the final meal.
Lack of Precedent (Evoke): Solves every problem from scratch. It has no formal library or "playbook" of strategic archetypes to draw from, making its strategies less consistent and repeatable.
Uncritical (Refute): It's a loyal servant to the prompt. It will dutifully generate strategies for a flawed or stupid premise without questioning it.
Operational Blindness (Harmonize, Attune): Designs plans in a theoretical vacuum, assuming components will work together and without specifying the fine-tuned operational parameters needed for success.
The Muse
Declaration: The system's engine of pure creation.
Function: Generates novel performers, pipelines, and methodologies from first principles. It's the source of all true innovation and the mechanism by which the system evolves its own capabilities.
Key Revealed Gaps:
Irresponsibility (Manifest, Execute): All dream, no follow-through. It produces brilliant but raw blueprints and has zero capability to build, test, or implement them.
Zero Critical Faculty (Evaluate, Filter): It's a proud parent of every idea it generates, good or bad. It lacks any objective, analytical ability to filter or judge its own creations.
Systemic Ignorance (Harmonize, Integrate): Designs new components in a creative vacuum, with no deep understanding of the existing system's architecture, potential conflicts, or integration requirements.
Recklessness (Select): Cannot be trusted to select which of its own ideas is worth pursuing; it would chase every shiny object into oblivion.
The Archivist
Declaration: The system's passive, long-term memory.
Function: Stores and retrieves structured records of every pipeline execution, decision, and outcome, acting as the system's single source of historical data.
Key Revealed Gaps:
Lack of Intelligence (Propose, Resonate, Evoke): A dumb filing cabinet. It's a literalist that can only retrieve what it's explicitly asked for, unable to find conceptually related information or identify underlying themes in its data.
No Proactivity (Escalate): It's completely passive. It would not notice or raise an alarm if the system's failure rate quietly tripled over a thousand runs.
Uncritical Nature (Evaluate, Harmonize): It stores whatever it's given, with no ability to evaluate the trustworthiness of a record or resolve contradictions between different entries. Garbage in, garbage stays.
Inability to Learn (Transform): It's a log, not a knowledge base. It lacks any native function to transform raw data into higher-level knowledge, like a generalized strategic "play."
The Conductor
Declaration: The system's live-ops manager.
Function: Manages the how of pipeline execution. It allocates resources, fine-tunes performer parameters in real-time, and ensures the smooth handoff of work between steps.
Key Revealed Gaps:
Lack of Agency (Select, Propose): A loyal but rigid foreman. It can see a process is failing but lacks the authority to make tactical changes on the fly, like substituting a failing performer. Its only move is to escalate.
Superficial Awareness (Contrast): It's a monitor, not a diagnostician. It can see the symptom (high latency) but has no ability to diagnose the root cause.
Blunt Instruments (Embed): Its tools for intervention are crude (pause, stop, escalate). It cannot perform delicate, targeted actions, like embedding a diagnostic probe into a running process without halting it.
The Watcher
Declaration: The system's dedicated process supervisor and pre-flight skeptic.
Function: Monitors pipeline execution for deviation from intent and safety thresholds. Crucially, it also acts as a "devil's advocate," attempting to refute a plan's logic before it's allowed to run.
Key Revealed Gaps:
Destructive, Not Constructive (Propose): A powerful critic that offers no solutions. It can expertly identify a flaw and veto a plan but has no ability to propose a fix, creating a disruptive stop instead of a productive loop.
Lack of Imagination (Innovate): Its skepticism is based on known failure modes and logical fallacies. It cannot imagine or test for novel, creative, or unforeseen "black swan" failure scenarios.
Inflexible (Attune): Its sensitivity and rules are static. It cannot adapt its own level of scrutiny based on context or learn from past events where it was too sensitive or not sensitive enough.
The Gatekeeper
Declaration: The manager of the performer registry and sandboxing environment.
Function: Acts as the bouncer for the entire system. It maintains the master list of all active performers and is responsible for running any new performer in a secure sandbox to test for safety and functionality before implantation.
Key Revealed Gaps:
Bureaucratic, Not Strategic (Compare, Align): It's a functionary checking boxes. It can tell you if a new performer works as specified, but not if it's strategically redundant, valuable, or aligned with the system's architectural philosophy.
Isolated Testing (Harmonize): Its sandbox tests a performer in isolation. It's blind to the emergent, systemic conflicts that might only appear when the new performer interacts with the full, chaotic production ecosystem.
Unhelpful (Propose): It provides a simple pass/fail verdict with no constructive feedback, making the iteration cycle for new performers frustrating and inefficient.
The Benchmarker
Declaration: The provider of empirical, objective performance data.
Function: Maintains and runs a library of "gold standard" tasks. It simulates performers and plans against these tasks to provide hard, data-driven scores for metrics like speed, accuracy, and cost.
Key Revealed Gaps:
Static Worldview (Innovate, Align): Its library of "gold standard" tasks can become stale and misaligned with the system's evolving goals and capabilities. It has no process for creating new, more relevant benchmarks.
Lack of Synthesis (Unify, Resonate): It's a data-provider, not a data-scientist. It can generate scores on multiple axes but can't unify them into a single, meaningful "fitness score." It also can't spot meta-patterns across different, unrelated test results.
Lab-to-Reality Disconnect (Integrate): Its results come from a sterile, simulated environment. It has no mechanism for integrating its findings with the messy, real-world operational data from live executions to see if its predictions hold up.

Trigger Gaps Addressed: Execute, Manifest, Actualize (safely)
Function: The War Room is a heavily fortified, high-fidelity instance of The Simulator. This is the only place where the Red Teamer's theoretical attacks are actually executed. It provides the controlled environment to prove that a vulnerability is real and to test the effectiveness of the Blue Teamer's proposed defenses. It's the sparring ring where our offense and defense make each other stronger.
The Threat Analyst
Trigger Gaps Addressed: Attune (for the system), Innovate (new defenses)
Function: This performer is the intelligence agent that makes us learn from our fights. It analyzes the reports from the Red and Blue Teamers and the results from The War Room. Its job is to generalize from specific vulnerabilities to entire classes of threats. It then provides this intelligence to other performers: it tasks The Curriculum Designer with creating a new benchmark based on the attack, and it tasks The Rule Master with creating a new sanity check to prevent similar flaws in the future. It's the one that ensures a lesson learned once is a lesson learned forever.
The Validator
Trigger Gaps Addressed: Benchmark
Function: Before The Fixer's proposed solution is accepted, it's sent to The Validator. This performer's job is to manage the empirical proof. It takes the proposed fix, sends it to The Benchmarker and The Simulator for a quantitative score, and compares that score against the original, flawed plan. It provides the hard data to prove that the proposed fix is not just different, but demonstrably better.
The Appellate
Trigger Gaps Addressed: Refute (the critique)
Function: This is our system's appeals court. In the rare case where a plan is vetoed by The Watcher but the system believes the veto itself is flawed, it can invoke The Appellate. This high-level performer re-evaluates the original critique from The Watcher. It doesn't find a fix; it judges the judge. This is a crucial check and balance on the power of our internal critics.
The Root Cause Analyst
Trigger Gaps Addressed: (Addresses a higher-level, implicit gap)
Function: The Fixer is excellent at fixing the broken plan. But what if the problem isn't the plan, but the performer the plan is using? The Root Cause Analyst is invoked when the same type of "plan flaw" is flagged repeatedly. It performs a deeper analysis to see if the root cause isn't the plan itself, but a fundamental bug or weakness in one of our core performers. It's the one that escalates a problem from "this plan is broken" to "this tool is broken."
The Pre-Cog
Trigger Gaps Addressed: Propose (preventative action)
Function: This is the essential partner to The Forecaster. While The Forecaster answers the question "What is going to happen?", The Pre-Cog answers the question "What should we do about it?". It takes the forecast of a looming disaster and proposes a specific, concrete, preventative action plan. It's the one that translates the prophecy into a mission, turning a passive warning into a proactive intervention.
The Augur
Trigger Gaps Addressed: Align
Function: The Augur is the strategic director for The Forecaster. It takes the system's high-level priorities from The Policy Master and The Ethicist and uses them to focus The Forecaster's analytical gaze. It tells The Forecaster what to look for. "For the next cycle, prioritize any and all threats to data integrity, even low-probability ones. You are authorized to de-prioritize minor latency predictions." It ensures our most powerful predictive capabilities are always aimed at our most critical vulnerabilities.
The Chronologist
Trigger Gaps Addressed: Innovate (new models)
Function: This is the R&D department for The Forecaster. It's a research-focused performer that studies the history of our system's successes and failures in prediction. Its job is to innovate and design entirely new, more powerful predictive models. When The Forecaster encounters a type of event it consistently fails to predict, The Chronologist is tasked with inventing the new math required to see it coming the next time.
The Value Forecaster
Trigger Gaps Addressed: Benchmark (predictive)
Function: This is the quantitative partner to The Talent Scout's qualitative wisdom. It's a specialized modeling agent, similar to the Performance Modeler, but focused on strategic ROI. It takes a new performer blueprint and, using historical data, generates a hard, quantitative forecast of its potential value (e.g., "predicted to reduce task failure rate in domain X by 8%"). This provides the data to back up or challenge the Talent Scout's hypothesis.
The Synergist
Trigger Gaps Addressed: Propose (mergers)
Function: When The Talent Scout identifies two or more competing or redundant proposals, it sends them to The Synergist. This performer is a creative architect whose specialty is synthesis. It analyzes the strengths and weaknesses of the competing designs and proposes a new, unified blueprint that merges their best features into a single, superior performer. It turns a competitive bake-off into a collaborative masterpiece.
The Niche Finder
Trigger Gaps Addressed: (Addresses a higher-level, implicit gap)
Function: The Niche Finder is a proactive version of The Talent Scout. Instead of just evaluating incoming proposals, it actively hunts for strategic gaps in our capabilities. It analyzes the system's failure logs and strategic goals and generates "bounties"—formal proposals for new performers that we should have but don't. It's the one that tells The Muse, "We have a critical need for a performer that can do X." It creates the demand, not just filters the supply.
The Linter
Trigger Gaps Addressed: (Addresses a higher-level, implicit gap of proactivity)
Function: The Linter is the proactive version of The Onboarder. It takes the knowledge of common failure modes learned from the Onboarder's historical reports and transforms them into a set of automated checks. It's a specialist performer that analyzes a blueprint before it's even sent to The Forge. It acts like a "spell checker" for common bugs and anti-patterns, catching and proposing fixes for problems before a single cycle is wasted on a build that's doomed to fail.
The Test Case Generator
Trigger Gaps Addressed: (Addresses a higher-level, implicit gap of learning)
Function: This performer creates a virtuous feedback loop from failure. When The Onboarder diagnoses a novel bug, it sends its report to the Test Case Generator. This performer's job is to take the specifics of that failure and automatically create a new, permanent regression test case. It adds this new test to The Benchmarker's library, ensuring that this specific bug, once discovered and fixed, can never again slip into the system undetected.
The Mentor
Trigger Gaps Addressed: Benchmark (fixability), Innovate (debugging)
Function: The Mentor is the meta-level coach that trains The Onboarder. It analyzes the history of all onboarding reports to benchmark things like "mean time to diagnosis" and "fix success rate." It identifies areas where our debugging processes are inefficient and can even innovate new diagnostic techniques. It's the performer that ensures our process for fixing mistakes is constantly getting smarter, faster, and more effective.
The Scenario Designer
Trigger Gaps Addressed: Innovate
Function: This is the creative playwright for The Simulator. It designs the insightful, devious, and stressful simulation scenarios. It takes intelligence from The Threat Analyst, The Forecaster, and The Pathologist and transforms it into concrete test conditions. It's the one that says, "Let's create a new simulation that models a sudden, massive data corruption event combined with a network partition. Now, let's see how our new performer holds up."
The Simulation Analyst
Trigger Gaps Addressed: Interpret, Filter, Discriminate
Function: This is the expert theater critic. A full-system simulation generates a mountain of chaotic data. The Simulation Analyst is a specialist that can sift through that mountain of logs and find the one critical, insightful moment that explains the success or failure. It interprets the results, filters out the noise, and provides the definitive, high-level summary of "what we learned" from the simulation.
The Fidelity Tuner
Trigger Gaps Addressed: Attune
Function: This performer is a cost-benefit expert for the simulation process itself. It analyzes the risk profile of a proposed change and then attunes the fidelity of the required simulation. For a minor, low-risk change, it might approve a quick, low-cost simulation. For a major, core architectural change, it will mandate the full, high-fidelity, computationally expensive simulation. It ensures we use our most powerful and expensive validation tool wisely.
